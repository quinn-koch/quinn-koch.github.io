Research & Projects

Senior Thesis · Ongoing

Pre-Industrial Wildfire Regimes in the Western US

Reconstructing historical fire return intervals in the western United States by integrating paleoecological records (tree-ring fire scars from the North American Fire Scar Network) and expert fire data (USGS Landfire) with the coupled climate-forest ecosystem-fire models DYNAFFOREST and WULFFSS, run on NCAR supercomputing infrastructure Casper and Derecho. The ultimate goal of this work is to produce realistic sumulations of regional-scale climate-fire-ecosystem dynamics during the pre-suppression era in the western United States. Advised by Dr. Park Williams, UCLA Geography.

NASA SARP · Summer 2025

Post-Fire Ecosystem Recovery via Spectral Indices & Flux Towers

Used Landsat multispectral data and eddy-covariance flux tower records to quantify carbon flux recovery trajectories in post-fire forest ecosystems at 5 FLUXNET sites in the United States and Australia. Completed a rapid introduction to the full scientific research process – branstorming, data acquisition, analysis, and presentation – over the course of a rigorous 7-week program. Presented at the American Geophysical Union Conference 2025, New Orleans, LA. advised by Dr. Lisa Haber and Dr. Brandon Alveshere (VCU).
